## Highlights
- I’ve written before that productivity is closely entangled [with the fear of death](https://moretothat.com/multi-tasking/). Without the awareness of our mortality, there may be no need for a shield to protect our hours from the onslaught of circumstance. If I spent an entire month distracted on social media but thought that the hourglass replenishes infinitely, it’s quite possible I wouldn’t feel bad about it. But because we recognize that our hourglass has a finite number of turns, we will bemoan that month as an epic waste of time. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01g1nm6gx9kq9yhx2rrqp4q23r))
- Simply put, **productivity is a set of systems or rituals that protect us from future regret**. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01g1nm6x98sh3d2gbs3w574g9s))
- The fundamental tension is this: The more you view your time through the lens of productivity, the less you can see it through any other lens. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01g1nm7qtwr2j7754r9gm9gr94))
- Burnout is often associated with working too much, but the real reason it happens is because you have defined yourself by what you produce. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01g1nm895eq6ys78sfdp924e7g))
