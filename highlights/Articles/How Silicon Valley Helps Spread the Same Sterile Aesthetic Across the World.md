## Highlights
- In his 1992 book *Non-Places*, Marc Augé, the French anthropologist, wrote that with the emergence of such identity-less space, "people are always, and never, at home." If we can be equally at home everywhere, as Roam and Airbnb suggest, doesn’t that mean we are also at home nowhere? ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01fzhfhrstmkmxqqzz4tmdqd0h))
- Yet the AirSpace aesthetic that Airbnb has contributed to, and the geography it creates, limits experiences of difference in the service of comforting a particular demographic ("the vanilla tourist") falsely defined as the norm. It is a "hallucination of the normal," as Koolhaas writes. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01fzhfr41k9gyp41xqvz7jtkx5))
- Seeking out difference is important, particularly when technology makes it so easy to avoid doing so. ([View Highlight](https://read.readwise.io/read/01fzhfttpvejv56qqwh9m58x82))
